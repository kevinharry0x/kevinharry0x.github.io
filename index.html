OP::BUGDETECTOR

Automated bug detection engine that crawls and identifies OWASP vulnerabilities. Reduces manual recon time by ~40%.

$ python bugdetector.py --url target.com
[*] Crawling endpoints → 212 URLs
[!] SQLi at /product?id=10

OP::KERNELSCAN

Kernel-level vulnerability scanner that checks for misconfigurations and privilege escalation vectors.

$ sudo kernel_scanner --deep
[!] CVE-2023-2640 detected
[✓] Remediation steps saved

IOT::FORESTSCAN

Firmware analysis & IoT security audit framework — scans for default creds and insecure services.

$ iot_scanner --target 192.168.1.0/24
[⚠️] default password detected
the journey · origin
chapter I · the awakening

🔐 My cyber journey truly began in my first year of college. I had always been curious about hacking, but I didn't know where to start. That's when I met Hemanth Karal Varmaa. I asked him for advice, and over time we became close friends — he became my mentor and teacher. He introduced me to his circle, which included seniors who were already deep into the field.In time, I became interested in bug bounty, and I didn't know how to research — and that's where I met Malathi (senior) and Rishyendra (senior). They helped me learn new things, and we became so close — especially with Malathi, with whom I formed a brother-sister bond.

chapter II · the turning point

Hemanth helped me land an internship at IIT — and that's where everything changed. At IIT, I met two extraordinary seniors: Kabil Preetham and Amithab. Both are incredibly talented and brilliant minds. Kabil, though much senior, is the kind of person who treats everyone equally and speaks without ego. We had many online sessions filled with learning and genuine fun. “Amithab is equally knowledgeable, but I'll admit — I had (and still have) a certain fear when talking to him. That very fear pushed me to learn more deeply.

“A guide lights the path, but the walker must choose never to stop.””
chapter III · the eternal student

If I hadn't met them, my life would have been a looping day without direction. My knowledge is still far from enough, but I will never stop learning. This is only the beginning.

The people you meet shape your path. Grateful to Hemanth, Kabil, Amithab, Risyendra, Malathi, and everyone who guided me.
